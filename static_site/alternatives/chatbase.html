1. The Credit Problem: Why Chatbase Gets Expensive Fast

One of the biggest complaints among Chatbase users is the opaque credit system. When you sign up, you're allocated a monthly credit budget. The problem is that different AI models consume credits at wildly different rates. If you use GPT-4o — the model that actually produces quality answers — your credits can disappear in a matter of days on a mid-tier plan.

This creates a frustrating dilemma: use a cheaper model and get mediocre answers, or use the best model and pay significantly more than the advertised plan price.

  • Chatbase: Credits deplete faster with GPT-4o queries. High-volume support bots can exhaust a monthly plan in under two weeks.
  • YoppyChat: Flat-rate pricing tied to your use case — not per-message credit consumption. Predictable monthly cost regardless of query volume.

💡 Real cost example: A WhatsApp support bot handling 500 conversations/month would exhaust Chatbase's $49 plan in roughly 10 days using GPT-4o. With YoppyChat's flat-rate model, your monthly cost stays constant regardless of conversation volume.

2. Hallucinations vs. True RAG Architecture

When building an AI clone of yourself or your business, accuracy is everything. You cannot afford for the AI to "make up" phantom product features or false opinions about topics you've never covered.

Chatbase is built on a fine-tuning paradigm with RAG layered on top — which sounds great, but in practice this hybrid approach can cause the model to blend your knowledge base with its own pre-trained biases. The result is confident-sounding but factually wrong answers.

  • Chatbase: Frequently struggles with "conversational drifting" — the bot gradually forgets to stay grounded in your documents and starts generating plausible-sounding but unsourced content.
  • YoppyChat: Rejects fine-tuning entirely in favor of pure Retrieval-Augmented Generation. Every answer is retrieved from your uploaded documents first. The LLM is only used to format the response — it cannot inject knowledge you haven't provided.

For a YouTube creator building an AI persona, this means your bot will never claim you said something you didn't. For a business, it means your support bot will never promise a refund policy that doesn't exist.
